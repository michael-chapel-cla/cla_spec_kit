# Go To Market — ExpenseFlow

## Best first channel

**Finance directors and HR managers at 50–200 employee professional services and field services companies.**

These buyers have direct authority, clear pain (monthly close friction, policy violations, reimbursement delays), and a budget line for software. They do not need IT approval for a per-user SaaS tool under £500/month. They share pain points openly in peer networks and finance forums.

## Wedge offer

**Free 30-day trial — full product, up to 25 users, no card required.**

The wedge is the submission-to-approval cycle. If finance runs one month-end close on ExpenseFlow and exports a clean CSV without manually chasing anyone, they do not go back. The trial converts at the point where the CSV replaces the spreadsheet.

## Sequenced go-to-market

### Stage 1: Proof (months 1–3)
- 5–10 design partners — finance directors sourced from founder network and LinkedIn outreach
- High-touch onboarding: founder-led, 1-hour setup call per tenant
- Goal: validate that first tenants use the product every month-end without prompting
- Pricing: free or heavily discounted in exchange for detailed feedback sessions

### Stage 2: Early revenue (months 4–6)
- Launch on Product Hunt and relevant finance/ops community newsletters
- Content: two or three comparison articles targeting "Expensify alternative mid-size company" and "SAP Concur too expensive" search intent
- Direct outbound: 30-50 targeted LinkedIn messages per week to finance directors in ICP industries
- Goal: 10 paying tenants at £149/month (up to 50 users)

### Stage 3: Referral and channel (months 7–12)
- Referral programme: existing tenants get one month free per referred company that converts
- Accountant / bookkeeper channel: offer a partner dashboard for accounting firms to manage multiple client tenants — firms with 10+ SMB clients become distribution partners
- Goal: 50 paying tenants; 20% of new tenants from referral or accountant channel

## Sales motion

**Low-touch PLG with a high-touch fallback.**

- Primary: trial signup → self-serve onboarding → card on file → automated → month-end retention hook
- Fallback: if trial tenant has not set up their first policy within 5 days, founder or CS sends a 20-minute onboarding call offer
- Enterprise fallback: for companies over 200 employees asking about SAML or custom SLAs, move to assisted sales with a commercial conversation
