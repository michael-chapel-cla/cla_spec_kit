# Pricing & Positioning — ExpenseFlow

## Positioning statement

ExpenseFlow is the structured expense management platform for mid-size companies that have outgrown email and spreadsheets but do not need — or cannot afford — an enterprise T&E suite.

**For**: finance directors and operations managers at 50–500 employee companies  
**Who**: spend too many hours per month chasing receipts and manually reconciling expenses  
**ExpenseFlow**: replaces ad-hoc email workflows with structured submission, policy enforcement, and one-click export  
**Unlike**: SAP Concur (too complex, too expensive) and Expensify (consumer-grade, card-focused)  
**Because**: we are purpose-built for out-of-pocket reimbursement with configurable policies — no card change required

## Pricing rationale

- **Per-company flat fee** (not per-seat) is the correct unit for this buyer. Finance directors care about company cost, not per-head cost. Flat fee also removes friction for adding users.
- **Caps at tier limits** rather than hard blocks — companies that grow into the next tier get a natural upsell conversation at renewal.
- **Annual discount (20%)** is the primary monetisation lever. Most SMB SaaS in this range converts 40–60% of paying customers to annual within 12 months.
- **Free trial**: 30 days, full product, up to 25 users — converts at month-end when finance sees a clean CSV for the first time.

## Value drivers by tier

### Starter — £79/month
Replaces the spreadsheet. A finance director spending 8 hours/month on manual reconciliation at £60/hour is losing £480/month in FTE cost. Starter pays for itself if it saves 2 hours.

### Growth — £199/month
Policy enforcement at submission time removes the audit-and-reject cycle. At 100 employees, even one rejected-and-resubmitted report per week at 30 minutes combined cost is £1,200/year in FTE time — 6× the tier cost.

### Business — £449/month
Receipt OCR removes the most common submission friction. Multi-step approvals enable compliance for regulated industries (healthcare, financial services). Onboarding call compresses time-to-value for larger teams.

## Why they buy

- Finance directors buy to reduce their manual reconciliation burden and close books predictably
- HR and ops managers buy to avoid being the policy enforcer and approval bottleneck
- Company administrators buy because the alternative is building a process from scratch
- Employees don't buy — but their satisfaction with fast reimbursement drives retention of the paying tenant

## Risk

- Price sensitivity is low at Starter; moderate at Growth when CFO is involved in the decision
- Zoho Expense undercuts on price for companies already in the Zoho ecosystem — do not compete on price there, compete on workflow quality and Azure-native positioning
- Enterprise deals require assisted sales and a longer evaluation cycle; defer until Growth tier is proving retention
