# Customer Personas — ExpenseFlow

## 1. Emma — Field Sales Employee

**Role**: Account Executive, 3 years at a 150-person professional services firm

**Situation**: Emma spends £400–£800/month on client meals, travel, and accommodation. She pays out of pocket and submits a monthly claim. Today she emails scanned receipts to her manager, who forwards them to finance. Reimbursement takes 3–6 weeks and she frequently has to chase.

**Goals**:
- Get reimbursed within 2 weeks of submitting
- Know the status of her claim without chasing
- Spend less than 15 minutes a month on expense admin

**Frustrations**:
- Receipts get lost in email threads
- No visibility on whether her claim has been approved
- Occasional rejections because of missing receipts she swears she sent

**Behaviours**:
- Photographs receipts on her phone immediately after purchase
- Often submits expenses in batches at month-end, not weekly

**What she needs from ExpenseFlow**:
- Fast mobile-friendly submission with photo capture
- Real-time status updates (submitted → approved → reimbursed)
- Clear rejection reason so she can fix and resubmit

## 2. Daniel — Team Manager

**Role**: Operations Manager, responsible for a 12-person field team

**Situation**: Daniel receives 15–25 expense approval requests per month via email or Slack. He approves them informally and relies on finance to catch anything unusual. He spends 30–45 minutes per week on expense-related interruptions.

**Goals**:
- Clear all pending approvals once a week in under 20 minutes
- Flag unusual claims without being the compliance enforcer
- Not be interrupted for questions about claims he has already reviewed

**Frustrations**:
- No single place to see all pending claims — they come in from multiple channels
- Policy rules are in a shared doc he rarely reads
- Gets blamed when something slips through that finance later flags

**Behaviours**:
- Reviews on his laptop during quiet time, not on mobile
- Trusts his team but wants to spot-check unusual amounts

**What he needs from ExpenseFlow**:
- Single queue of pending approvals with totals and receipt thumbnails
- Approve or reject in one click with optional comment
- Automatic policy flagging so he does not need to apply the rules himself

## 3. Rachel — Finance Administrator

**Role**: Finance Manager at a 200-person healthcare logistics company

**Situation**: Rachel runs the monthly close. She spends 8–12 hours each month collecting expense submissions, chasing missing receipts, manually reconciling to the bank, and preparing data for payroll. She owns the company's expense policy but has no way to enforce it at submission time.

**Goals**:
- Close the books predictably at month-end without chasing
- Export clean, categorised expense data to payroll and accounting
- Enforce the policy without manually reviewing every line item

**Frustrations**:
- Employees submit expenses late, after the payroll cut-off
- Policy violations are only caught in manual review — too late to fix
- Finance data is in multiple spreadsheets with inconsistent category names

**Behaviours**:
- Heavy spreadsheet user — values clean CSV export above all else
- Owns the policy document and updates it quarterly
- Has final authority on whether to reimburse disputed claims

**What she needs from ExpenseFlow**:
- Policy enforcement at submission time — not on her desk
- Filtered, period-based views of all approved expenses by department
- One-click CSV export with consistent column structure
- Audit trail showing who approved what and when

## 4. James — Company Administrator

**Role**: Head of HR at a 90-person professional services firm

**Situation**: James manages onboarding, offboarding, and system configuration. He set up the previous expense process by writing a Word document and sending it company-wide. He is not technical but is responsible for ensuring the expense process works for everyone.

**Goals**:
- Configure approval chains when the org structure changes (quarterly)
- Add or remove users when people join or leave
- Not need IT support to manage the system

**Frustrations**:
- The current process breaks whenever a manager changes
- No clear owner when an approval is stuck
- Has to email finance to update the spreadsheet every time someone leaves

**Behaviours**:
- Not a power user — wants a settings page that is self-explanatory
- Delegates day-to-day questions to finance but owns configuration

**What he needs from ExpenseFlow**:
- Simple user management (add, deactivate, assign roles)
- Department and manager assignment without technical help
- Policy configuration UI — not a database or YAML file
