# Pitch — ExpenseFlow

Every mid-size company has the same problem: employees spend money, photograph receipts, and then nothing works reliably. The receipt gets emailed to a manager who approves it two weeks late via a reply-all chain, a finance analyst manually keys it into a spreadsheet, and the employee gets reimbursed — eventually — if the row did not get lost.

ExpenseFlow ends that loop. It is a structured expense management platform for companies with 50–500 employees: clean mobile submission, configurable approval routing, policy enforcement at the point of submission, and one-click export to accounting and payroll systems. The entire process — from receipt photo to reimbursement approval — takes under three minutes for the employee and under thirty seconds for the approving manager.

The wedge is the underserved mid-market gap. SAP Concur and Certify are too expensive and too operationally complex for companies that do not have a dedicated travel-and-expense team. Expensify and Ramp are consumer-grade or card-first, leaving companies that pay out-of-pocket and reimburse with no good structural option. ExpenseFlow owns that gap: more capable than a spreadsheet, deployable without a consultant, priced for a 200-person company.

The moat is workflow lock-in and data. Once approval chains, policy rules, and department structures are configured, switching costs are meaningful. Over time, the platform accumulates spend pattern data that enables a second product layer: spend analytics, policy recommendations, and anomaly detection that point-solution competitors cannot replicate without the same history.

The market is large and the buyer is obvious. Every company with employees that spend money is a prospect. The go-to-market is finance director and HR director-led — two roles with clear authority, clear pain, and clear budget to solve it.
